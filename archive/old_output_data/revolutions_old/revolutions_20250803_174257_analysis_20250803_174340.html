Top Causal Chain Visualization

Analytical Summary of Top Chain (LLM):

The end of the French and Indian War in 1763, which left Britain with significant debt, served as the initial trigger for a series of escalating colonial tensions. British attempts to impose new taxation and control over western lands led to widespread discontent, culminating in protests like the Stamp Act and Townshend Acts, followed by violent incidents such as the Boston Massacre and Boston Tea Party. These events prompted punitive British responses, including the Intolerable Acts, which solidified colonial unity and led to the First Continental Congress. The subsequent Battles of Lexington and Concord ignited open warfare, leading to the Declaration of Independence and ultimately, the decisive victory at Yorktown and the Treaty of Paris in 1783, establishing the sovereign United States.
