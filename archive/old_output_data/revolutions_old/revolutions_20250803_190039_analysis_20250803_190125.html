Analytical Summary of Top Chain (LLM):

The American pursuit of independence was primarily triggered by the conclusion of the French and Indian War in 1763, which led Britain to impose new taxes and assert tighter control over the colonies. This sparked growing colonial discontent and organized resistance, escalating through a series of legislative actions like the Stamp and Townshend Acts, and direct confrontations such as the Boston Massacre and Tea Party. The British response with the Intolerable Acts further fueled the movement, culminating in the First Continental Congress and the Battles of Lexington and Concord, initiating the Revolutionary War. The formal Declaration of Independence in 1776 solidified the cause for sovereignty, which was decisively secured through military victory at Yorktown and formally recognized by the Treaty of Paris in 1783, establishing the United States.
