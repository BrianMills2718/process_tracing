Analytical Summary of Top Chain (LLM):

The primary causal chain leading to American independence commenced with the British imposition of new taxes and control following the 1763 French and Indian War, notably the 1765 Stamp Act, which sparked immediate colonial resistance. This escalated through punitive British responses like the Intolerable Acts, consistently fostering greater colonial unity, evidenced by the First Continental Congress and the formation of militias. The escalating political tensions culminated in open armed conflict at Lexington and Concord in 1775, ultimately leading to the formal Declaration of Independence in 1776 and the definitive military victory at Yorktown, culminating in the 1783 Treaty of Paris establishing the sovereign United States.
