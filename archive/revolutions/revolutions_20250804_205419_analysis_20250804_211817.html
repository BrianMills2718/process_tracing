Analytical Summary of Top Chain (LLM):

The American Revolution's causal chain began with Great Britain's substantial debt from the French and Indian War (E1), prompting Parliament to impose new taxes like the Stamp Act (E2) and Townshend Acts (E3) on the colonies. This direct taxation, coupled with a lack of representation, escalated colonial discontent, leading to acts of resistance such as the Boston Tea Party (E4) and subsequent punitive Intolerable Acts. These British retaliations unified the colonies under the First Continental Congress (E5), eventually culminating in the formal Declaration of Independence (E6) and ultimately, American victory and the Treaty of Paris (E7) establishing a sovereign United States.
