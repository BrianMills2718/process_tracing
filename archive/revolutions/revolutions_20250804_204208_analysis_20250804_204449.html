Evidence Strength Comparison

Analytical Summary of Top Chain (LLM):

The conclusion of the French and Indian War in 1763, which significantly increased Great Britain's national debt, served as the primary trigger for a series of escalating colonial tensions. British attempts to generate revenue through direct taxation, such as the Stamp Act and Townshend Acts, provoked widespread colonial resistance, boycotts, and organized protests like the Stamp Act Congress and Boston Tea Party. London's punitive response, exemplified by the Intolerable Acts, further unified the colonies, leading to the First Continental Congress and the outbreak of the Revolutionary War in 1775. This culminated in the Declaration of Independence in 1776 and the ultimate recognition of U.S. independence through the Treaty of Paris in 1783.
