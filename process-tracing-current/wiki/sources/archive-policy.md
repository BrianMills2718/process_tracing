# Summary

The archive policy establishes two OKF-style wiki bundles: a current repo wiki in
the active project and an archive wiki under `~/archive/process_tracing`. The
current repo wiki may be used for orientation. The archive wiki is consulted only
for history, recovery, previous attempts, or archive cleanup.

# Key Rules

- `wiki/log.md` is the chronicle location for both bundles.
- Archive material should leave `~/projects/` after review.
- Archive material is non-authoritative unless promoted back into active docs,
  code, plans, or tests.
- Agents must not search the archive during normal implementation work.

# Interpretation

This policy separates three questions that are easy to blur: where retired files
physically live, where historical meaning is summarized, and what counts as
current authority. Files can be preserved in the archive, but that preservation
does not make them active guidance. The archive wiki can explain why something
was retired or why it might be worth recovering, but promotion still has to occur
through the active repo's normal governance path.
