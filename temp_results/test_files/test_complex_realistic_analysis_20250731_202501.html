Analytical Summary of Top Chain (LLM):

The Cuban Missile Crisis unfolded as a causal chain initiated by the Soviet Union's deployment of nuclear missiles in Cuba (October 1962). This action, discovered by U.S. intelligence, triggered a series of high-stakes Excomm deliberations that resulted in the implementation of a U.S. naval quarantine of Cuba. The crisis culminated in the Soviets' agreement to remove the missiles on October 28, 1962, resolving the immediate threat of nuclear conflict. This sequence demonstrates a clear causal link between Soviet actions, U.S. response, and the eventual crisis resolution.
