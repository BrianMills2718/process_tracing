Methodology and Assumptions

Analysis Approach

This analysis employs Bayesian process tracing combined with Van Evera diagnostic tests to evaluate causal hypotheses. The methodology integrates:

  • Van Evera Diagnostic Tests: Evidence classified as hoop tests (necessity), smoking gun tests (sufficiency), doubly decisive tests (both), or straw-in-the-wind tests (weak evidence)
  • Bayesian Inference: Systematic belief updating using likelihood ratios and prior probabilities
  • Confidence Assessment: Multi-dimensional confidence evaluation including evidential, causal, coherence, robustness, and sensitivity factors
  • Uncertainty Analysis: Monte Carlo simulation to quantify uncertainty and test robustness

Key Assumptions

  • Evidence Independence: Evidence pieces are assumed to be conditionally independent unless specified otherwise
  • Likelihood Assessments: Probability assessments are based on Van Evera templates adjusted for evidence strength and reliability
  • Prior Probabilities: Initial probabilities assigned based on available information and expert judgment
  • Causal Mechanisms: Analysis assumes identifiable causal mechanisms linking causes to effects

Limitations and Considerations

  • Analysis is limited by the quality and completeness of available evidence
  • Probability assessments involve subjective judgment and may vary across analysts
  • Causal inference remains probabilistic and cannot establish absolute certainty
  • Results should be interpreted in context of broader theoretical and empirical knowledge
